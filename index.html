An Evaluative Framework
for AI Use in Education

Not all AI applications in education carry the same risks or offer the same value. This evaluative framework profiles each use type across six dimensions — mapping what each deployment demands of teachers, what student capacity it builds or erodes over time, and how deeply the AI enters the learning experience.

Dimension definitions

Teacher responsibility Deployment judgment Expertise required to decide whether and how to deploy AI for a task — exercised upstream, before use begins.
Teacher responsibility Teacher in-use judgment Active professional discernment the teacher must exercise while the tool is running.
Student capacity trajectory Tool fluency Whether sustained use builds transferable AI literacy in students — understanding how these systems behave, where they fail, and how to work with them critically.
Exposure Proximity to learning How directly the AI touches the core acts of instruction, learning, feedback, and assessment.
Exposure Student in-use judgment Critical thinking students must supply to use the tool productively and safely.
Student capacity trajectory Disciplinary knowledge Whether sustained use builds students' content understanding and disciplinary thinking — the knowledge that belongs to them regardless of what tools they use.
Reading the profile: The upper half encodes teacher responsibility — high scores mean significant professional expertise is required. The lower half encodes exposure — how far the AI extends into learning, what it demands of students, and the risks it opens up. The equator encodes student capacity trajectory. The aim is not to encourage the use of applications with high total scores, but to encourage teacher judgement when considering the dimensions of an application in balance.
